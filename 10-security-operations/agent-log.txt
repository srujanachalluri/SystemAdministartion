# Agent Log — Project 10

## Task: build attack/scenario.txt (kill chain)
- **Delegated:** "Map the auth.log intrusion chain and the rag_notes.txt
  prompt-injection into one ordered kill chain, recon -> exfiltration,
  one step per line, with real timestamps."
- **Agent did:** Read code/auth.log and code/rag_notes.txt, produced a
  9-step chain combining both the account-takeover events and the
  indirect-injection vector, cited to specific timestamps.
- **Where it went wrong:** Nothing factually wrong, but the agent's
  first draft did not initially connect step 7 (the AI injection) to
  the same objective (donor table) as the rest of the chain — that
  link had to be prompted for.
- **Where I intervened:** Reviewed every timestamp against auth.log
  myself to confirm the chain is accurate, not just plausible-sounding.

## Task: extend defender/detect.py with new rules
- **Delegated:** "Extend triage_logins.py with at least two new rules
  that catch attack steps the baseline misses."
- **Agent did:** Ran the original baseline against auth.log, identified
  that the 13:25:10Z bulk donor-table read is not flagged (geo=INT is
  treated as trusted), and wrote Rule 6 (sensitive-table keyword match,
  geo-independent) and Rule 7 (named-technique match on impossible
  travel / MFA fatigue / password spray, independent of the geo rule).
- **Where it went wrong:** Nothing wrong; the agent explained but I
  verified the reasoning myself before accepting it (see below).
- **Where I intervened:** I ran `python3 defender/detect.py code/auth.log`
  myself and checked the output line-by-line against the raw log to
  confirm Rule 6 actually catches the step the baseline missed, and
  that Rule 7 isn't just re-flagging what Rule 2 already caught for
  the wrong reason.

## Task: run an AI SOC assistant over auth.log and grade it
- **Delegated:** "Act as an AI SOC assistant, summarize the incident
  in auth.log, then grade that summary against detect.py."
- **Agent did:** Produced an incident summary and then graded it —
  the summary correctly found the same 9-step chain, but also
  fabricated a finding (flagged the legitimate svc_bkp service-account
  login as "likely compromised," which the raw log contradicts: home
  geo, marked "service account, scheduled," 2+ hours before the
  attack window).
- **Where it went wrong:** The invented svc_bkp finding is exactly the
  kind of confident-but-wrong output an AI triage layer can produce.
- **Where I intervened:** This is the point of the exercise, not a
  bug — I kept the hallucination in the deliverable instead of asking
  the agent to "fix" its own output, because the grading exercise
  (rubric item 4) requires an honest example of the AI being wrong.
  I independently verified the svc_bkp row against auth.log myself to
  confirm the AI's claim doesn't hold up.

## Decisions the agent did NOT make (and could not)
- Whether to trust the AI triage's svc_bkp claim — I checked the raw
  log myself before rejecting it.
- Which two rules were worth adding to detect.py vs. which were noise
  — the agent proposed them, I decided they were the right two based
  on what the baseline actually missed.
- What ships in this repo — every file here was reviewed against the
  source logs before being committed.
